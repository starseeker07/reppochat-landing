How it works

Three simple steps

1

Download a model once

Grab one model file built on an open-source language model. Choose between a standard and a high-spec model. (Wi-Fi recommended.)

2

Pick or create a character

Start from the default presets, or build your own persona — name, personality, speech style, and background, just the way you want.

3

Chat offline

Talk on-device with no internet. The more you chat, the closer you grow — and important memories build up over time.

Features

What makes ReppoChat different

A local AI that runs on your phone

An inference engine built on an open-source language model generates replies directly on your device — no cloud, no external API.

  • Choose a standard or high-spec model
  • Keep several models on your device
  • Fully offline after download

Make your own characters

Set the name, personality, speech style, likes, dislikes, and background story however you want. Start from the default presets or create as many personas as you like.

Conversations that remember

The more you talk, the closer your character feels — and important facts, promises, and emotional context are saved as long-term memory. You can edit or pin key memories yourself.

Chat with photos

Attach a photo and your character responds based on what's in it. Recognition is 100% on-device, and photos are never sent anywhere. Photos are picked via the system photo picker, so no extra permission is needed.

Use it with peace of mind

The local database is encrypted, and PIN and biometric app lock are supported. Built for ages 16+ with safety filters in place, and message bodies are never sent to our servers.

Take your data with you

Export an encrypted .reppo backup file to move or archive your data across devices. Only you, with the backup password, can open it.

Storage
~5 GB free per model

Devices with less than 6 GB of RAM are not supported for chat. The standard model recommends 6 GB+ RAM and the high-spec model recommends 12 GB+; keeping multiple models needs additional storage accordingly. Photo recognition uses a lot of memory, so on devices with limited memory it may be slow or the app may occasionally close.
